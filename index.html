Cyclistic Bikeshare – Case Study

By Mesula Korsa • Portfolio Edition

Executive Summary

Why this matters. Annual members are more profitable than casual riders. The goal was to understand behaviour differences so Cyclistic can convert casuals to members and improve service where it counts.

What the data shows. Members account for ~64% of trips. Usage peaks in July–August, dips in January. Casual riders cluster on weekends (leisure) while members show a weekday commute pattern (notable Tuesday spike). Classic & e‑bikes dominate; docked bikes are rarely used by members.

What to do. Focus conversion in Mar–Aug with a Summer‑Pass→Annual ladder, a Weekend‑Only pilot, and a Commute bundle that rewards weekday streaks. Operationally, increase e‑bike availability and tighten rebalancing around weekend/summer surges; reassess docked bikes in member‑dense areas.

Data source: 12 monthly CSVs (Jan–Dec 2023). Cleaning in Power Query; star schema with Trips fact + Calendar/Station dimensions; DAX for measures.

Recommendations

  • Marketing & pricing (Mar–Aug): Summer Pass→Annual, Weekend‑Only pilot, and a commute bundle (reward AM/PM streaks).
  • Service & operations: Target rebalancing at Fri PM/Sat AM (leisure) and weekday peaks (commute). Raise e‑bike share pre‑season.
  • Measurement: Primary KPI = casual→member conversion. Secondary = availability SLA, ride frequency; North‑star = 12‑month retention/LTV.

Ethics: public Cyclistic capstone dataset; no personal identifiers. For health projects, follow HIPAA de‑identification and FAIR data practices.
